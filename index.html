Postal History of the Gulf Region

  • 5th Anniversary of Sheikh Khalifa’s Accession – 1977

    On the occasion of the 5th anniversary of Sheikh Khalifa’s accession to the throne Qatar issued a set of two stamps. The values issued were 20 Dirhams and 1.80 Riyals.

  • The 1977 Definitive Issue

    Qatar issued its 6th definitive set of stamps in 1977 depicting Sheikh Khalifa bin Hamed Al-Thani. A total of 7 stamps were issued with the following values: 5D, 10D, 35D, 80D, 1R, 5 Rupees and 10 Rupees.

  • 25th Anniversary of the Arab Postal Union – 1977

    To celebrate the occasion of the 25th anniversary of the Arab Postal Union Qatar issued a set of two stamps depicting the emblem of the APU. The values issued were 35 Dirhams and 1.35 Rials.

  • The 1st Qatar booklet – 1977

    Qatar issued its first booklet in 1977 consisting of a total of 10 stamps. The values issued were 5 Dirhams (four copies), 10 Dirhams (3 copies), 35 Dirhams (2 copies) and 80 Dirhams.

Postal History of the Gulf Region

  • King George VI Definitive Issue - 1951

    The second definitive set issued by the British Postal Agency in Bahrain. It comprises of 9 stamps and was issued on the 3rd of May 1951 with the exception of the 4 Annas stamp, which was issued on the 2nd October 1950. The values issued were: ½ Anna – 1 Anna – 1 ½ Anna – 2 Annas – 2 ½ Annas – 4 Annas – 2 Rupees – 5 Rupees and 10 Rupees.

  • The 1977 Definitive Issue

    Qatar issued its 6th definitive set of stamps in 1977 depicting Sheikh Khalifa bin Hamed Al-Thani. A total of 7 stamps were issued with the following values: 5D, 10D, 35D, 80D, 1R, 5 Rupees and 10 Rupees.

  • The 1st Local Issue – 1953/1956

    Bahrain issued its first independent stamp on the 15th of March 1953 bearing a portrait of the then ruler, Sheikh Salman bin Hamad Al Khalifa. The value of the stamp was 1 ½ Anna, which was the internal letter rate at the time. The stamp was the first of a set of three and the other two values - ½ Anna and 1 Anna - were issued in October 1956.
